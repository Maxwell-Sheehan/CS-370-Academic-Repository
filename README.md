Connect your learning from throughout this course to the larger field of computer science:
What do computer scientists do and why does it matter?
Largely speaking, computer scientists are given problems, and solve them using a combination of software and hardware to tackle them. It is a very broad field that has different emphasis on different subjects, some groups focus more on hardware, other software. Either way the research and development matter as it allows us to improve the world and gradually contribute to people having more access to tools, and resources. 

How do I approach a problem as a computer scientist?
Throughout the course I've gradually learned that the best way to approach a problem is to break it down into smaller and smaller pieces. That way you can make the problem a lot more approachable, and tackle it with bite sized pieces. You slowly disect the issue, and through continuous effort you end up making a lot of progress in an issue that might seem increadibly daunting. 

What are my ethical responsibilities to the end user and the organization?
In anything we design we want to make sure that it is secure. That we take security and safety of data to the individual and orginazition very seriously. It is our responsibilty to ensure we treat other peoples concerns for privacy and safety in the software that they use with the same seriousness we would our own. With machine learning and AI specifically, I think we have an ethnical obligation to reduce the bias that can be instilled in these models.
